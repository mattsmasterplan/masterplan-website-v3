Dan Case Build

March 2019

Black anodized aluminum and a minimalistic design led me to choose Dan Case. 7.2 Liters is not a lot of volume for computer components, but I was able to build a solid computer that will serve as my workstation for many years. Most impressive is how tight the tolerances were on this mini ITX board. The CPU cooler has 1mm clearance on all sides. Despite this, there is no contact and everything fits as expected. My key component is the 970 PRO M.2 SSD. This incredibly fast 1 TB drive is smaller than a stick of RAM and mounts directly to the motherboard. Custom length power supply cables help airflow and prevent heat buildup. An i7-8700 was chosen because it will not get too hot for this cooler to handle. Vents allow heat to rise out of the top of the case and I have normal operating temperatures. I used foam tape to attached the wifi antenna to the back of the case.

Case: DANCases A4-SFX v3
Motherboard: ASUS ROG Strix Z390-I Mini ITX
CPU: Intel Core i7-8700
CPU Cooler: Noctua NH-L9i
GPU: Nvidia GTX 1080 Founders Edition
Memory: Corsair Vengeance LPX 16GB DDR4 3000MHz
Storage: Samsung 970 PRO 1TB NVMe M.2 SSD
Samsung 850 EVO 500GB 2.5" SSD
Power Supply: Corsair SF600 Platinum
Power Supply Cables: Pslate Customs
Monitors: 2x LG 27" 4K HDR (27UK600-W)
Monitors Stand: Kanto DMS2000
